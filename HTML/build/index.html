About Me

A common thread in my life and work has been understanding and helping people.

Professional Background:


Designer and Educator


For over the last 3 years, I've worked as a freelance and contract designer with clients including Ecommerce, Education, Healthcare, Finance, Nonprofit, Agency, and HVAC. In these projects, I've worn many hats, and executed (primarily) web projects from ideation to execution and delivery. It's been a privilege to do this work, and my current goal is to become a long-term contributor rather than freelancer.

For more work examples, I've proudly built the following websites: Echolux Creative, Berkeley Music Instrument Exchange, Living Arts Playback Theatre Ensemble, and Juan Balikbayan.

Prior to that, I spent 10 years in a fast-paced digital media college as an instructor and lecturer for music, audio, video and web. During this time, I taught a range of courses from audio editing and sound design to video production, and multimedia assembly. I still teach private lessons and perform locally from time to time, but most of my energy at this point goes to my UX Design work.

Other Interests:


Music, Language, Political Science and History


Music is my first profession. I have learned the basics of harmony, rhythm and these have led me to UX Design through seeking ways to be "in step" with other people.

I love languages. These days, I primarily use English but Spanish and Tagalog are others that occur more or less daily. In the past, I have had touchpoints with a fair number of languages, but some other highlihgts are German, Mandarin Chinese, Thai, and French.

My degree in Political Science helps me make informed decisions as a citizen, and interpret policy changes as they come and go.

Improvements are made through a better understanding of history. I love going back to moments in history to learn even more, and also bring that persistence to understanding the crux of things in each UX Design project.

My commitment to excellence in my design career means bringing value to others, because I am genuinely curious to know how best I can be of assistance.
